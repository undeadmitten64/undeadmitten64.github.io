The Sister

by Sung-Yoon Lee

8.7 / 10.0

I really loved this book, it kept me engaged until the very end, even though it's a non-fiction essay. It might be partly because of my avid interest in North Korea, but also the writer is incredibly masterful in his words, and really sees through propoganda of all countries (not just North Korea) really well. I picked up this book because I wanted to learn more about Kim Jong-Un's sister, since she could be the heir to the "throne" of the DPRK. Even if you're not too into non-fiction essays I think you can get a kick out of this book if geopolitics interests you at all, although the book can be quite depressing as most things about North Korea are. This is a very solid book.
